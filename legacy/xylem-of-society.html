01
The Premise
Xylem

Optimise on what already works

Nature has been running distributed optimisation for billions of years. Xylem lets agents reason like it does — this page asks what happens when a society does.

A nature proxy is not a metaphor borrowed for decoration. It is a working principle — pheromone trails, slime-mould networks, branching river flows — abstracted into rules that coordinate, adapt and allocate.

If these principles can route agents through a 3D task, the wager of this page is simple: they might also route value through an economy.

02
Voltaire & Abundance
Voltaire

The age of commerce, defended

In 1736, against moralists mourning a lost golden age, Voltaire thanked nature for placing him in an age of commerce.

His Le Mondain reframed trade — and even luxury — as engines of refinement: circulation that funds the arts, multiplies wants and joys, and binds distant peoples together. Growth, for Voltaire, was not sin but civilisation.

The provocation we keep is his appetite for abundance. The provocation we add: change what we grow, and how the harvest is shared.

Keep the appetite for abundance. Change what we grow.

03
The Chagra
Chagra

An economy of cultivation

The Amazonian chagra is a working economy that has run for millennia — without extraction.

Diversity over monoculture. Cyclical productivity over depletion. Reciprocity over accumulation. It is an in-situ bank of thousands of species, a system where what is taken is returned.

Read as economics, the chagra measures success by regeneration — the exact opposite of an economy that consumes its own base.

04
Model · Measure · Mentor
XylemChagra

Nature applied to markets

The Symbiotic Code reads nature as model, measure and mentor. Markets can be read the same way.

Model — copy the structure of living networks. Measure — benchmark against the resilience of ecosystems, not GDP alone.

Mentor — let nature set the ethical floor: assistance, not dependency; cooperation, not domination; optimisation that does not eclipse meaning.

06
Agentic AI
Xylem

Rehearse the economy before living it

Before a policy touches a person, it can be run thousands of times in simulation.

Xylem's deterministic proxy and decision bridge become a sandbox for economic policy: seed it, run it 1,550 times, watch where collisions, idle capacity and re-crossings appear.

Then choose the layer that sustains the most resilience. AI as a rehearsal space — not an oracle.

07
The Global South
Global SouthChagra

New spaces to grow into

Developing economies need not repeat the extractive industrial path to reach abundance.

With local-first connectivity and nature-proxy coordination, regions like the Amazon can leapfrog. CHAGRA-NET sketches the corridor — Leticia to Belém — where biomimicry becomes governance and value-allocation.

It aligns with COP30's bioeconomy agenda and a single principle: forest protection and direct community benefit must move together, or not at all.

08
The Necessary Caution
VoltaireGlobal South

The offensive of capital against life

Critical economists name our moment bluntly — and the warning belongs here.

Financialisation, technological control and the management of crisis through coercion are real. Nature proxies can be captured too — optimised toward extraction as easily as regeneration.

A proxy is only credible when communities are co-designers and rights-holders, never just inspiration. Abundance bought against the living base is not abundance — it is a loan against the future.

09
The Synthesis
XylemChagraVoltaire

Value that flows like sap

Picture the economy as a plant's vascular system — the Xylem of Society.

Roots feed the xylem · the xylem lifts value to the canopy · the canopy drops it back to the roots

Roots are communities and biodiversity. Xylem is the coordinating tissue — AI and trade carrying value upward. The canopy is shared abundance that, like a forest, drops its leaves back down to feed the roots.

Voltaire's growth, the chagra's regeneration and Xylem's coordination meet in one circulatory model: cultivated, layered, relational.

A provocation, not a prescription

This is a beginning, not a blueprint.
